Extracurricular Clubs and Activities
Participation in these is voluntary. Students and parents must understand that these activities are not covered in the general expenses of the school. To participate, there are financial obligations for the parents. Depending upon the club or activity, the cost for the student will vary. Should the group experience success, it may require extra travel and expense to continue in competition. The school is not responsible for these incurred expenses and the burden will rest with the parents and students. It is also not the responsibility of the staff sponsor or the school to raise the money. Each club or activity will have a fee to participate. This fee is used to cover the cost to compete, t-shirts, uniforms, official fees, and other expenses. Any additional travel costs will be covered by the student/parent.
Clubs and Fees:
Battle of the Books $15 (includes team t-shirt) – 5th Grade only
Chess Club
Clay Class – Independent Fee to Instructor
Kidpower – Outside Company Fee
Landsharks $75 (includes t-shirt)
Legos Club – Outside Company Fee
Matchwits $40
Math Club No Fee except Travel Expense covered by student
MIDI Keyboarding – Outside Company Fee
National Junior Honor Society $25 dues, $20 t-shirt – 7th & 8th grade by invitation only
Science Matters – Outside Company Fee
Student Council No Fee – elected positions (polo shirts optional fee)
Taekwondo – Outside Company Fee
Traveling Sea – Outside Company Fee
Young Rembrandts – Outside Company Fee
*Possibility exists that the teams advance to state or national competitions. All costs incurred will be the responsibility of the student/parent.

John Irwin School of Excellence Award Winner
The Colorado Department of Education has announced that for the 12th consecutive year Monument Academy has been named a John Irwin School of Excellence Award Winner.  
This prestigious award is given to the top 8% of Colorado Public Schools. Schools that demonstrate excellent academic achievement “exceeding” expectations on the state’s academic achievement indicator are recognized.
